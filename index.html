A tenacious Asian American student currently enrolled at the University of California, Riverside (UCR), pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Neuroscience, I am deeply committed to unraveling the intricacies of the human brain. Within the realm of neuroscience, I have been honored with a Research Scholarship, a testament to my commitment to advancing the field through rigorous exploration. As a Research Assistant at the UCR Neuroscience Lab, I am engaged in cutting-edge projects focusing on specific areas such as synaptic plasticity or neurodegenerative diseases. This role has allowed me to apply and refine advanced laboratory techniques, including immunohistochemistry and electrophysiology, while contributing meaningfully to the lab's ongoing investigations. Adept at data analysis using tools like SPSS and programming languages such as Python, I have presented my findings at departmental seminars, enhancing both my analytical and communication skills. Beyond academia, I have assumed leadership roles, currently serving as the President of the UCR Neuroscience Club. My commitment to fostering community extends beyond the club as evidenced by my past role as an Orientation Leader in the UCR New Student Orientation Program, guiding incoming students through their transition to university life and ensuring they were well-informed about campus services, academic expectations, and extracurricular opportunities. Proficient in various neuroscience laboratory techniques, including PCR and Western blotting, I possess a well-rounded skill set that extends beyond the laboratory. As a member of the Society for Neuroscience (SfN), I stay abreast of the latest research and advancements in the field, further fueling my passion for neuroscience. Looking forward, my aspirations include pursuing a Ph.D. in Neuroscience, where I intend to delve deeper into specific areas of interest, such as neuroplasticity, with the ultimate goal of contributing groundbreaking research that advances our understanding of the complexities of the brain.
